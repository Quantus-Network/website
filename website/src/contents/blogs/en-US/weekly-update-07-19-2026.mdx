Our redesigned block explorer is live at [explorer.quantus.com](https://explorer.quantus.com).

![Quantus blockchain explorer redesign homepage](/blog/assets/quantus-blockchain-explorer-redesign-homepage.webp)

Quantus allows two types of transactions: transparent and encrypted.

Transparent transactions look like any other blockchain transaction.

Encrypted transactions (currently possible using the CLI but coming to our mobile app soon) use wormhole transfers.

A wormhole transfer happens in two steps.

First you send coins to a wormhole address. That address is a hash of a hash of a secret only you know, so nothing can ever spend from it. As far as the chain is concerned, you burned them. Later you hand the chain a zero-knowledge proof that says I know the secret behind one of the funded wormhole addresses, without revealing which one, and it mints your coins to a fresh address.

So the explorer shows you everything that exists: coins going in, coins coming out, the amounts, the proof. The one thing it can't show you is which entry matches which exit, because that link never touches the chain.
