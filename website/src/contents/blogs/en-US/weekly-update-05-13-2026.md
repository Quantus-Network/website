Early Bitcoin miners could mine thousands of BTC a day on a laptop.

Bitcoin CPU mining was ONLY viable for basically 2 years.

As the network grew, so did hashrate. GPU mining became more viable, then ASICs.

By the time you first heard about BTC, mining on consumer hardware was already dead.

There was no exchange to buy on.

Since there was no secondary market, there was no "price."

To get a "price," you estimated electricity costs per coin.

By the time you could market buy, most the coins had been distributed.

Bitcoin emitted over half its coins in the first four years.

All of it went to early miners.

So that's the standard for a "fair launch".

We can probably do better.

When we prepare to launch [@QuantusNetwork](https://x.com/@QuantusNetwork), we're prioritizing making mining accessible from day one.

You can get a mining node running on our testnet today in a few clicks.

Last week we updated our GUI miner app. Even if you're not technical, you can be ready for mainnet mining on day one.
